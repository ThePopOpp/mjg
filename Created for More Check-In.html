Welcome

Welcome from Michael

Most people do not drift because they do not care. They drift because life gets full. Responsibilities grow. Calendars fill. Work expands. Family needs attention. Financial pressure builds. Health gets pushed aside. Joy becomes something we hope to get back to someday.

And somewhere along the way, many people stop asking one of the most important questions they could ever ask:

What kind of life am I actually building?

The Created for More Check-In is not designed to make you feel guilty or overwhelmed. It is not a test you pass or fail. It is a simple reflection tool to help you pause, look honestly at the direction of your life, and identify one area that may need your attention next.

You do not need to fix everything today. You simply need to begin noticing. Because awareness is where stewardship begins.

I approach this work from a Christian faith perspective because that is the lens through which I have learned to understand life, purpose, family, work, money, and legacy. But this guide is faith-based, not faith-exclusive. Wherever you are starting from, you are welcome here. The invitation is simple: slow down, think deeply, and begin paying attention to the life you are building.

The Case for Reflection

Why this check-in matters

01

Busyness can disguise drift

Most people are not doing nothing. They are doing everything. The danger is not usually laziness — it's motion without direction.

02

Success does not always equal alignment

A person can be successful and still be misaligned. Productive and still exhausted. Responsible and still reactive. Impressive from a distance and unsettled up close.

03

Reflection is where transformation begins

Before you can redesign your life, you have to see your life clearly. You cannot steward what you refuse to examine.

This check-in will help you evaluate five key areas of your life — Purpose, Family, Fitness, Fun, and Finances — which connect to the larger Stewardship Blueprint framework: faith, purpose, relationships, health, joy, resources, guardrails, habits, energy, and legacy.

Instructions

How to use the check-in

Set aside 10 to 15 quiet minutes. You can complete this quickly, but do not rush through it. The value is not in the score alone. The value is in the honesty.

For each statement, rate yourself from 1 to 5.

ScoreMeaningDescription
1Strongly DisagreeThis is rarely true in my life right now.
2DisagreeThis is occasionally true, but not consistently.
3Neutral / UnsureThis is somewhat true, but I have room to grow.
4AgreeThis is mostly true in my life.
5Strongly AgreeThis is consistently true and intentionally protected.
Answer based on what is actually true, not what you wish were true.

Your calendar, habits, energy, relationships, spending, and recurring thoughts often tell the truth before your intentions do.

Score Tiers

What your score may be telling you

100–125of 125

Aligned and Intentional

You appear to be living with a meaningful level of clarity and intentionality. The next step is not complacency — it is protection. What is healthy should be guarded, strengthened, and sustained.

"What rhythm, relationship, habit, or priority do I need to protect?"
75–99of 125

Aware but Stretched

You likely have meaningful alignment in some areas, but you may also feel stretched, reactive, or inconsistent. This is a common place for responsible people. You are not off track, but busyness may be beginning to create drift.

"Where am I giving too much attention to urgency and not enough to importance?"
50–74of 125

Drifting in Key Areas

Your score may indicate that parts of your life are being shaped more by default than by design. This is not a reason for shame. It is a reason to pause, pay attention, and begin making intentional adjustments.

"Which area of my life is asking for my attention right now?"
< 50of 125
