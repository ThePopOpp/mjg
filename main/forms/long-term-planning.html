Faith, identity, and your best life

Start with the life you believe God is calling you to live. A financial plan becomes more powerful when it begins with who you are, what you believe, and what flourishing truly means to you.

Best-life reflection

Before planning forward, it helps to notice where life has felt most alive, what you would improve, and what wisdom you have gained from the road behind you.

Purpose, priorities, and alignment

Most people drift toward urgency. This step helps identify where your current life and financial decisions support your best life, and where a better blueprint may be needed.

Rest, relationships, and rhythm

A financial plan should support a life that has room to breathe: gratitude, Sabbath, family, friendship, reflection, recreation, and peace.

Resources for the life you are called to live

Stewardship includes more than account balances. This section helps your planning team see the full picture of what can support your best life, your family, your giving, and your future impact.

Investing with wisdom and conviction

Investing can be more than accumulation. It can help fund your best life, express patience and prudence, support generosity, and align resources with the kind of future you want to help build.

Decisions, fear, and faith

Many decisions are shaped by love or fear. This step helps name what is driving your choices and invites faith, wisdom, and boundaries into the planning process.

Generosity and kingdom impact

A best life is not only measured by what you keep. Giving is a central expression of stewardship, gratitude, trust, and love for people.

Guardrails, protection, and wisdom

The right guardrails protect the life you are building: your faithfulness, family, calling, generosity, peace, and ability to remain steady through uncertainty.

What happens after you submit?

Your SIG planning team will review your responses, identify the highest-impact planning areas, and prepare a focused conversation around your best-life vision, biblical stewardship, financial security, wise investing, generosity, and long-term legacy.

This planning form is a discovery tool and does not provide individualized financial, legal, tax, or insurance advice. Your SIG advisor will use your responses to guide a personalized planning conversation.
