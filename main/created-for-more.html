Welcome

Welcome from Michael

Most people do not drift because they do not care. They drift because life gets full. Responsibilities grow. Calendars fill. Work expands. Family needs attention. Financial pressure builds. Health gets pushed aside. Joy becomes something we hope to get back to someday.

And somewhere along the way, many people stop asking one of the most important questions they could ever ask:

What kind of life am I actually building?

The Created for More Check-In is not designed to make you feel guilty or overwhelmed. It is not a test you pass or fail. It is a simple reflection tool to help you pause, look honestly at the direction of your life, and identify one area that may need your attention next.

You do not need to fix everything today. You simply need to begin noticing. Because awareness is where stewardship begins.

The Case for Reflection

Why this check-in matters

01

Busyness can disguise drift.

Most people are not doing nothing. They are doing everything. The danger is not usually laziness — it’s motion without direction.

02

Success does not always equal alignment.

A person can be successful and still be misaligned. Productive and still exhausted. Responsible and still reactive.

03

Reflection is where transformation begins.

Before you can redesign your life, you have to see your life clearly. You cannot steward what you refuse to examine.

This check-in will help you evaluate five key areas of your life — Purpose, Family, Fitness, Fun, and Finances — which connect to the larger Stewardship Blueprint framework.

Instructions

How to use the check-in

Set aside 10 to 15 quiet minutes. You can complete this quickly, but do not rush through it. The value is not in the score alone. The value is in the honesty.

Score Meaning Description
1 Strongly Disagree

This is rarely true in my life right now.

2 Disagree

This is occasionally true, but not consistently.

3 Neutral / Unsure

This is somewhat true, but I have room to grow.

4 Agree

This is mostly true in my life.

5 Strongly Agree

This is consistently true and intentionally protected.

Answer based on what is actually true, not what you wish were true.

Section
01

Purpose & Direction

Five Statements • 25 Points

Core Question Am I living with clarity about what kind of life I am building?

I regularly slow down to reflect on the direction of my life.

I have clarity about what matters most in this season.

My calendar reflects my stated priorities.

I am building my life around purpose, not just pressure or expectation.

I can identify where I may be drifting and what needs my attention.

Section
02

Family & Relationships

Five Statements • 25 Points

Core Question Are the people who matter most receiving my best?

I am intentionally investing in my closest relationships.

The people I love are receiving my presence, not just my provision.

I have healthy rhythms for connection with family, friends, or community.

I am leading, serving, or encouraging the people entrusted to me.

I am not allowing busyness to consistently crowd out relationships.

Section
03

Fitness & Energy

Five Statements • 25 Points

Core Question Am I stewarding my body and energy well enough to live with purpose?

I am caring for my physical health with intention.

I have enough energy to be present for what matters most.

My sleep, movement, nutrition, and recovery support the life I want to build.

I recognize when I am depleted and need renewal.

I see my health as capacity for calling, service, and stewardship.

Section
04

Fun, Joy & Meaningful Experiences

Five Statements • 25 Points

Core Question Am I making room for joy, adventure, rest, and meaningful experiences?

I make time for activities and experiences that bring joy and renewal.

I schedule meaningful moments instead of hoping they happen accidentally.

I am creating memories with the people I love.

I allow space for rest, celebration, adventure, or play.

My life includes more than responsibility, productivity, and obligation.

Section
05

Finances & Stewardship

Five Statements • 25 Points

Core Question Is money serving my purpose, or shaping my identity?

I see money as a tool, not the ultimate goal.

My spending reflects my values and priorities.

I have clarity about what financial stewardship looks like in this season.

I am using resources for provision, generosity, freedom, and impact.

I am not allowing fear, comparison, status, or accumulation to define success.
